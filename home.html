Ministry of AYUSH

The Ministry of AYUSH, established in 2014, focuses on promoting traditional Indian systems of medicine, including Ayurveda, Yoga, Unani, Siddha, and Homeopathy. With a vision to enhance the quality of life, it aims to integrate these traditional practices into the national healthcare system. The ministry actively works towards research, education, and policy-making to strengthen these systems, ensuring their relevance in modern healthcare. Looking ahead, the Ministry of AYUSH aspires to globalize these traditional practices, making them accessible to people worldwide while maintaining their authenticity and efficacy.

Unani

The Unani system of medicine has its origins in ancient Greece and was further developed in the Middle East and South Asia. It is based on the teachings of Hippocrates and Galen and incorporates the principles of four humors.

Unani emphasizes the importance of a balanced lifestyle, diet, and the use of herbal remedies. It focuses on the body's natural healing processes and offers treatments for various ailments.

Yoga

Yoga is an ancient practice that combines physical postures, breathing techniques, and meditation to promote holistic health. Originating in India over 5,000 years ago, yoga aims to unite the mind, body, and spirit.

Practicing yoga can enhance flexibility, strength, and mental clarity. It is widely recognized for its ability to reduce stress, improve concentration, and support overall well-being.

Homeopathy

Homeopathy is a system of alternative medicine developed in the late 18th century by Samuel Hahnemann. It is based on the principle of "like cures like," where substances that cause symptoms in a healthy person are used in minute doses to treat similar symptoms in illness.

Homeopathy emphasizes individualized treatment and aims to stimulate the body's self-healing abilities. It is used for various chronic and acute conditions, including allergies, migraines, and stress-related issues.
